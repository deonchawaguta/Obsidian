### What is Privacy Program Management? 
- The structured approach of combining projects into a framework and life cycle to protect personal information and the rights of individuals 

### What is program management?
- Managing multiple projects across an organisation to improve performance 

### What is a framework?
- The structure needed  to support program management

### What is a life cycle?
- The series of stages that something passes through during its existence

- The privacy life cycle and framework follow recognised program management principles and consider global privacy laws and regulations while implementing concepts such as Privacy by Design (PbD)

>[!Note] What is Privacy?
> - Privacy is a dynamic object with a discrete set of attributes and actions 
> - Privacy is **not** synonymous with secrecy and should not be confused for data classifications models  

- A privacy governance life cycle provides the methods to **assess, protect, sustain** and **respond** to the effects of influencing factors 

### The phases of the privacy operational life cycle 

#### Assess
- This phase provides the steps and processes necessary to assess any gaps in a privacy program as compared to industry best practices, privacy policy, laws and regulations 
- There are many models and [[Privacy Frameworks|frameworks]] that can be used to measure and align activities

#### Protect
- This phase provides the data life cycle, information security practices and PbD principles to protect personal information

#### Sustain
- This phase provides the monitoring, auditing and communication aspects of the management framework
- Monitoring should be continuous and based on risk appetite 

#### Respond
- The fourth of four phases that includes the respond principles of information requests, legal compliance, incident-response planning and incident handling 
- The aim is to reduce organisational risk and comply with regulations 
### Responsibilities of a Privacy Program Manager
- The roles and responsibilities vary depending on organisation

>[!important] The goals of a **Privacy Program Manager** are to:
> - Define privacy obligations
> - Identify and mitigate privacy risks 
> - Identify existing documentation around the management of personal information
> - Create, revise and implement policies and procedures that comprise a privacy program 
> - Raise the data IQ of the organisation to drive a privacy-oriented culture 

>[!Important] The goals of a **Privacy Program** are to:
> - Demonstrate a framework to enable compliance with applicable data protection laws and regulations 
> - Promote trust and confidence in the data entrusted by individuals
> - Highlight that an organisation takes its privacy obligations seriously 
> - Respond to privacy breaches and data subject requests 
> - Continually monitor, maintain and improve the maturity of the privacy program 

### Accountability 
- *Accountable* organisations have the proper policies and procedures to promote best practices in handling personal information and can demonstrate compliance with applicable privacy laws 
- They need to take ownership of personal information throughout the data life cycle

#### Beyond Law and Compliance 
-  Motivators beyond law and compliance can include consumer trust, this is difficult to quantify however unlike fines and fees it can be difficult to regain once it has been lost 

### Why is a Privacy Program needed?

>[!Important] Reasons for having a privacy program include:
> - Enhancing an organisation's brand
> - Meeting regulatory obligations
> - Encouraging ethical processing practices 
> - Enabling global operations e.g., M&As 
> - Mitigating the effects of data breaches
> - Providing a competitive differentiator 
> - Increasing the value and quality of data
> - Reducing the risk of lawsuits
> - Being a good corporate citizen 
> - Meeting client and consumer expectations
> - Integrating data ethics into decision-making 

### Privacy  Across the Organisation 
- Managing privacy within an organisation requires the contribution and participation across business functions such as HR and customer services 
- Members of a privacy team may sit within other functional groups and have a dedicated privacy role
- Buy-in and sense of ownership assist with better acceptance of privacy 
- Privacy policies should be created and implemented at a functional level

>[!Example] Examples of different functions involved in the creation of procedures related to privacy include:
> - The **Learning and Development** function enables policies and procedures to be translated into teachable content 
> - The **communications** team assists with the publishing of internal content that reinforces good privacy practices 
> - The **information security** team deploys security enhancing technology to help privacy meet its requirements for implementing security controls

### Championing Privacy 
- Protecting personal data cannot be the exclusive job of the privacy function
- Many organisations create a privacy committee comprised of *privacy champions* whose expertise in their areas of responsibility will inform strategic decisions and their implementation within their own departments
