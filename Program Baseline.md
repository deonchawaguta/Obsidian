## Program Baseline 
- Establishing a well defined baseline for the privacy program is a good start to determining current and desired success
- Metrics to consider include education and awareness, the regulatory environment, policy compliance, data management
- One should also consider risk assessment methods, incident management. compliance risk and program assurance 

### Education and Awareness 
- All employees should complete a basic privacy awareness education program 
- Advanced employee education involves role-based training 

### Monitoring and Responding to the Regulatory Requirement
- Organisations should have documented procedures for monitoring the regulatory environment and records that such monitoring has occurred 

### Assess Policy Compliance against Internal and External Requirements 
- It is important to talk to employees, look at records of program activities and assess changes and improvements made in response to metrics
- Inspect systems that store and process data

### Data, Systems and Process Assessment
- A baseline assessment must include an analysis of how data is managed.
- Is there a data minimisation procedures? Are there records management policies? 
### Maps Data Inventories, Flows, Life Cycle and System Integrations 
- *Data Lifecycle Management* has three objectives
	- Data security 
	- Data confidentiality 
	- Data integrity 
	- Data availability 
- It has 5 stages 
	- Collection 
	- Storage, Organisation and Backup/Recovery
	- Usage, Sharing and Processing 
	- Archiving
	- Destruction 

### Risk Assessment Methods 
- The most common methods for conducting privacy risk assessments include:
	- Privacy threshold assessments
	- Privacy impact analysis 
	- Data Protection Impact Analysis
	- Legitimate Interest Analysis
	- Transfer Impact Analysis 
 - These assessments identify specific risks and based on those risks, quantify the likelihood of an incident 
### Incident Management, Response and Remediation 
 - Procedures should be in place as required by the privacy program framework 
 - They should be fully documented and include roles and responsibilities 

### Gap Analysis against an Accepted Standards or Law 
- After completion, the baseline must be compared against a desired state, this must include compliance with applicable regulations. 

### Program Assurance 
- Program assurance review involve examining policies to ensure they are complete and respond to compliance obligations 
- They also check that policies are followed appropriately and completely


## Audiences for Metrics 
- *Primary* audiences have direct responsibility for relevant activities in the privacy program
- *Secondary* audiences are more concerned with broader strategy
- *Tertiary* audiences have no direct operational involvement but the organisation benefits from keep them informed 

## Privacy Metrics 

### Trend Analysis 
- Demonstrates the relationship between two variables 
- Typically tracking outcomes over time e.g. breaches or privacy incidents over time 

### Privacy Program Return on Investment (ROI)
- Looks at cost of implementing privacy protection as investment to avoid cost of potential privacy incidents 
- Calculations prioritise risk and assign values to each risk and factor the prevalence of the risk 
- Information's inherent value can also be weighed against the cost of protecting it if the organisation also sells data 

### Business Resiliency Metrics 
- Refers to the capabilities of an organisation to continue operations through adverse events 
- This metric relies on the organisation's readiness to respond to disasters
- Appropriate metrics in this area may include: 
	- Incident response drills conducted
	- Documented data recovery procedures 
	- Penetration testing
	- Drills conducted to test data recovery capabilities
	- Reviews of pre-planned disaster-related communication strategies

### Privacy Program Maturity Level 
-  A maturity model assigns levels of maturity to functional areas within a program 
- They don't assume every function needs to operate at the highest level but determine which functions have to operate at what level 
- Refer to [[AICPA Privacy Maturity Model]]

### Resource Utilisation
- Most useful to CFOs and CIOs. 
- To measure ROI, we must know the resources being consumed, including human resources
