- A framework to allow for data transfer between APEC member nations 
- Not a specific regulation or binding agreement 

## 9 Core Principles 
1. Preventing Harm 
2. Notice 
3. Collection Limitation
4. Uses of Personal Information
5. Choice 
6. Integrity of Personal Information
7. Security Safeguards
8. Access and Correction
9. Accountability 

- The framework also defines a *safe harbour* program based on it 
- This is called the Cross-Border Privacy Rules or CBPR system 
- This system is voluntary for member nations to join 
- It requires that organisations identify a government agency and at last on third party organisation to verify compliance for participating businesses 
- In the U.S. the Federal Trade Commission provides oversight and TrustArc verifies compliance
- Once certified by TrustArc, a U.S. business can conduct trade with an APEC member state


>[!important] **Safe harbour** programs may present weaknesses. They may hold participants to lower privacy standards. Stricter privacy standards may not be enforceable in all member states.
