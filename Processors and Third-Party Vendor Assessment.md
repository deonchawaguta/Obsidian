- Before trusting a third party with data, it is important to perform a detail evaluation of the processor's privacy program and identify any risks 

## Evaluate Processors and Third-Party Vendors 
- Third-party risk assessments often take the form of questionnaires that ask vendors to describe aspects of their privacy programs 
- If a vendor is unwilling to share, it may be beneficial to offer a mutual non-disclosure agreement
- Common aspects to assess are:
	- Privacy and Information Security Policies
	- Access Controls 
	- Where personal information is being held 

## Understand Sources of Information 
- In evaluating third parties, information may be taken from a number of sources such as
	- Internal Audit reports
	- Information and Physical Security programs, assessments and reports
	- Data Protection Authority public records of breaches
