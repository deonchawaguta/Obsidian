## Privacy Threshold Analyses 
- Often in the form of a questionnaire 
- Identifies if systems contain PII or other information that may require further analysis 
- May be used to determine whether further assessments should be conducted
- PTAs identify which data is held by which systems
- Often simpler and less costly than other assessment methods
- Should be done when considering new systems, when systems are repurposed or when information held in systems is unknown

## Privacy Impact Assessment 
- Required by the US DHS for all government agencies
- Triggered by changes in an organisation that impact privacy protections 
- A PIA has three goals according to the U.S. DHS
	- Ensure conformance with legal requirements 
	- Determining risks and effects 
	- Evaluating protections and alternative process to mitigate potential privacy risks 
- PIAs help to consider the implications of changing data privacy infrastructure 

## Data Protection Impact Assessment 
- A type of PIA required by the EU GDPR 
- Required for all organisations covered by the GDPR 
- Triggered when changes to data processing is likely to impact privacy protections
- At a  minimum they must include:
	- Description of the proposed changes
	- Assessment of how much data processing is necessary to accomplish the purpose 
	- Assessment of data privacy risks 
	- Plans to mitigate any increased data privacy risks 

## Legitimate Interest Assessment 
- Conducted by organisations subject to the EU GDPR 
- GDPR requires that an organisations have a 'legitimate interest' to conduct data processing activities lawfully 
- LIA establishes legal basis for data processing by documenting stated purposed and proposed processing

## Transfer Impact Assessment 
- Performed when engaging in internal data transfers 
- Ideally a PIA or DPIA should have already been done 
- The TIA adds the requirement to analyze the laws of the country to which the data will be transferred
